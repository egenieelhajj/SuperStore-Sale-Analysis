# Superstore Sales Analysis

Excel | Pivot Tables | Dashboard | Business Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes retail sales data from a US-based superstore across multiple years (2015–2017). Using pivot tables and charts in Excel/Google Sheets, the goal was to identify top-performing regions, product categories, customer segments, and sales trends over time to support strategic business decisions.

Dataset: Thousands of retail transactions including order details, customer segments, product categories, regions, and sales figures.

## Business Problem

Retail businesses need to understand where their revenue is coming from and how it changes over time. This analysis answers:

"Which regions, categories, and customer segments drive the most revenue, and where should the business focus its efforts?"


## Dashboard

The project includes an interactive dashboard with 4 visualizations built entirely with pivot tables and charts:

- Sales by Category —> Pie chart showing revenue distribution across product lines

- Sales by Region —> Bar chart comparing total sales across the 4 US regions

- Sales Trend by Month —> Line chart showing monthly sales performance over time

- Sales by Segment —> Bar chart breaking down revenue by customer type

4. Sales show an upward trend with strong year-end peaks

The monthly sales trend shows consistent growth from 2015 to 2017, with notable spikes at the end of each year, likely driven by holiday season purchasing. The most significant peak occurs in late 2017, suggesting strong business momentum.

## Business Recommendations

1. Double down on the West region — It's already the top performer. Increasing marketing or inventory investment there is likely to yield strong returns.

2. Investigate the South region — Revenue is significantly lower than other regions. This could be a market gap worth exploring or a signal to reallocate resources.

3. Leverage year-end momentum — Since sales spike consistently at year-end, the business should plan inventory and staffing around Q4 demand to maximize revenue during peak periods.

4. Analyze furniture profitability — Despite high sales volume, furniture margins should be reviewed. If profitability is lower than Technology or Office Supplies, the product mix strategy may need adjustment.
